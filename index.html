ABOUT BAPC
The Bay Area Programming Contest (BAPC)
is a programming contest for contestants of all levels designed by
Gunn's Competitive Programming Club. We hope to give Bay Area
high school students an opportunity to compete with peers and solve
some interesting problems in an ICPC style format.
FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
WHERE WILL BAPC BE HELD?
BAPC will be held in-person at Gunn High School's Titan Gym. There will be an online mirror for those who can't make it.
WHAT LANGUAGES ARE AVAILABLE?
We support the following languages: C++, Python3, and Java.
WHAT IS THE FORMAT OF THE CONTEST?
The contest will be a 3 hour long ICPC style contest. You will be given between 13 problems to solve with your team.
HOW MANY PEOPLE CAN BE ON A TEAM?
Teams may have up to 3 members.
